### Tips for Recording Demos

I have strong feelings about videos which have terminal windows.

1. Use a bigger font than you think; your text needs to be big enough to read in a large room when projected to a screen.
1. Use WHITE backgrounds, black fonts. Your cool dark-mode terminals don't have the contrast necessary to be read (see above).
1. Raise the bottom of your terminal window approx. 1" from the bottom of your screen. so many times people are craning their necks to see past the podium, etc, to see the very bottom-most quarter of the screen.
1. Finally (this is my personal preference) it's much better for you to have a script off on a second monitor and to carefully hand type any CLI commands you want to present. If you do anything else, I guarantee you the CLI commands will zip past too fast for the audience to actually process what you are trying to demonstrate. Tediously typing out the commands gives the viewer time to follow along as if they were watching over your shoulder.
1. Using AI to generate the voiceover is a good idea! The thing that convinced me is that you can edit the script and fix a goof, regenerate the voice track without having to re-record the whole thing. Then you can re-generate the final video + audio much more easily than trying to edit the audio track. It is also a great way to make it impossible for you to talk too fast.
